---
title: "Distill, Don't Repeat"
description: "The output of a sprint is synthesized knowledge, not raw artifacts. Create living documents that serve as the single source of truth."
---

## The Principle

The output of a sprint is not the raw artifacts themselves — the transcripts, the screenshots, the meeting notes, the Figma files. The output is the **synthesized knowledge** extracted from them.

This is a fundamental shift in how project documentation works. Instead of accumulating folders of raw material that nobody will ever re-read, the team creates and maintains **living documents** that serve as the single source of truth.

## Why This Matters

Every project suffers from **context burn** — the gradual loss of institutional knowledge as raw artifacts pile up and become impossible to navigate. A team that finishes a two-week sprint with 47 documents, 12 recordings, and 200 Slack messages has not created knowledge. They have created noise.

The cost is concrete:

- **Onboarding new team members** requires them to read through weeks of accumulated material
- **Returning to a decision** means hunting through meeting notes to find the original rationale
- **AI assistants** cannot effectively consume sprawling, unstructured project histories
- **Sprint planning** starts from scratch because the lessons from previous sprints are buried

## The Practice

At the end of every sprint (or at natural breakpoints during continuous work), synthesize all raw material into three canonical documents:

1. **DECISIONS.md** — Every significant decision, its context, alternatives considered, rationale for the choice made, and any dissenting opinions
2. **PERSONAS.md** — The current understanding of users, updated with any new insights from research, testing, or stakeholder feedback
3. **PRINCIPLES.md** — Design and engineering principles that have been validated or discovered during the sprint

These documents are **append-and-refine**, not **rewrite**. Each sprint adds to them, corrects them, or promotes uncertain observations to confirmed principles.

## The Test

After synthesis is complete, apply this test: **Can someone who missed the entire sprint read only the three living documents and be fully caught up?**

If the answer is no, the synthesis is incomplete. If the answer is yes, the raw artifacts can be archived — they have served their purpose.

## Anti-Patterns

- **The Archive Trap**: Saving everything "just in case" without synthesizing. The archive grows, but knowledge does not.
- **The Summary Email**: Writing a summary at the end of a sprint but not updating the canonical documents. The summary becomes stale immediately.
- **The Fresh Start**: Beginning each sprint with a blank slate instead of building on the living documents from the previous sprint.
